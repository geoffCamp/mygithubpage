I got my first taste of programing while enrolled in the Biological Engineering program at the University of Guelph. I continued to dabble in code throughout my studies and while working in Quality Engineering post graduation. After a brief, six month stint in manufacturing, I decided to answer my true calling; in 2014 I returned to UoG in the Bachelor of Computing program.

I did extensive C programming thoughout my Computing degree and was also introduced to object oriented concepts via Java. I was able to leverage that experience while creating the WeatherWare Android application and further while developing the Yumr platform for both Android and iOS. Aside from mobile development, I am also an adequate full stack web developer having completed two social web applications and a number of freelance website projects.

My goals for the near future are split between two different paths. I'm looking to either dive deeper into the world of software, becoming familiar with backend architecture; or to take a step back from programming and apply my coding skills to a more physical project such as mechatronics engineering. I find both directions to be equally intriguing and look forward to investigating them further as opportunities present themselves.
